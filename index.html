Deep dive: How do React hooks really work?

Hooks are a fundamentally simpler way to encapsulate stateful behavior and side effects in user interfaces. They were first introduced in React and have been broadly embraced by other frameworks like Vue, Svelte, and even adapted for general functional JS. However, their functional design requires a good understanding of closures in JavaScript. In this article, we reintroduce closures by building a tiny clone of React Hooks.
